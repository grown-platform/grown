Live

Self-hosted live streaming — broadcast from your browser or pro software, and let your team (or the public) watch in their browser.

Open Live → Browse all apps

Overview

Live turns your workspace into its own streaming channel. Create a stream and go live straight from the browser — sharing your webcam or your screen — or feed it from OBS and other broadcast software over RTMP. Viewers watch on a clean page over HLS, or over low-latency WebRTC. Streams can be visible to your organisation only or made public for anyone to watch.

Features

  • Go live from the browser. Publish straight from a tab over WebRTC (WHIP) — capture your webcam and mic, or share your screen, no software to install.
  • Stream from OBS. Each stream gets an RTMP ingest URL and a secret stream key for OBS and other broadcast tools.
  • Watch over HLS. Viewers play streams over HLS in any modern browser — robust across networks and devices.
  • Low-latency playback. A WebRTC (WHEP) read endpoint is available for near-real-time viewing when latency matters.
  • Org or public visibility. Keep a stream inside your organisation, or mark it public with a watch page anyone can open without an account.
  • Browse & filter. See what's live now, or filter to your own streams or all streams; titles, descriptions and a live badge keep the list scannable.
  • Stream lifecycle. Create, edit title/description/visibility, end a broadcast, and delete streams you're done with.

How it works

Live uses a self-hosted MediaMTX media server as its ingest/egress core, proxied under the workspace origin. Browser broadcasts publish over WHIP (WebRTC); OBS publishes over RTMP with a per-stream key. The same path is served back out as HLS for broad-compatibility playback and as WHEP (WebRTC) for low-latency viewing. Stream metadata and visibility live in the platform database.

Media core
Self-hosted MediaMTX (proxied under the workspace origin)
Ingest
RTMP (OBS, broadcast software) · WebRTC/WHIP (browser Go Live)
Playback
HLS (broad compatibility) · WebRTC/WHEP (low latency)
Auth
Workspace single sign-on (OIDC); public streams need no account
