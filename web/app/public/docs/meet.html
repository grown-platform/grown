Communication

Meet

Peer-to-peer WebRTC video meetings — your video goes straight to the other participants, with no server relaying your call.

Open Meet → Browse all apps

Overview

Meet is video conferencing built on WebRTC. Create a room, share its short code, and everyone connects directly to one another — audio and video flow peer-to-peer rather than through a central server. Controls for mic, camera, screen share, hand raise and in-call chat are all one click away.

Features

  • Peer-to-peer video. Each participant connects directly to the others over WebRTC; media never routes through a relay server.
  • Short room codes. Create a named room and get a short shareable code (like abc-defg-hij); join from the room list, by typing a code, or straight from a /meet/<code> link.
  • Participant grid. Tiles for every participant with name, mute and camera indicators, and an avatar fallback when a camera is off.
  • Mic & camera controls. Toggle your microphone and camera; your state is reflected to everyone in the room.
  • Screen sharing. Share your screen with one click; the shared track replaces your camera across all connections and stops cleanly when you end it.
  • Hand raise. Raise your hand to signal the room; a badge appears on your tile.
  • In-call chat. A side panel for text messages during the call, with an unread badge.
  • Roster panel. A slide-out list of everyone in the call with mute, camera and raised-hand status, plus a live participant count.
  • Copyable invite link. Copy the meeting URL from the top bar to invite others.
  • Graceful join. No camera or mic? You can still join to see and hear the others.

How it works

A websocket signaling channel coordinates the call: it exchanges WebRTC offers, answers and ICE candidates, and relays control events like join, leave, mute, hand raise and chat. Once peers are connected, audio and video travel directly between them. STUN is used to traverse NAT; the actual media stays peer-to-peer.

Media
WebRTC, peer-to-peer (no server relay of audio/video)
Signaling
Websocket per room (SDP offers/answers, ICE, control events)
Connectivity
STUN for NAT traversal
Auth
Workspace single sign-on (OIDC)
